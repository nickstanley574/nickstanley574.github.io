The other day I talked to a former colleague about what makes a good engineer in addition to technical and communication skills. The conversation centered around how people handle being wrong and making mistakes.

To be human is to be imperfect, the best thing to do is embrace being wrong. Being wrong means you are about to learn something new and correct a misconception that could happen again in the future. A good engineer, and human in general, knows and this takes it in stride. 

It's like my grandmother used to say "You only stop making mistakes and learning when you stop breathing."
